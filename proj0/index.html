Project 0: Becoming Friends with Your Camera

An intuitive exploration of camera perspective, focal length, and the center of projection.

Overview

In this project, we want to become friends with every pixel. The first step toward this goal is to become friends with our camera—e.g., the one in your smartphone. In particular, the goal of this project is to get some intuitive understanding of the somewhat subtle relationship between perspective, focal length/zoom, and the center of projection.

Part 1: Selfie: The Wrong Way vs. The Right Way

Explanation

When taking photos with a camera closer-up, you have a shorter focal length which creates wide-angle views. Thus, this can exaggerate and distort parts of you image that are closer to the lens. When taking photos from a distance, the longer focal length creates more depth and a well-balanced natural selfie.

Part 2: Architectural Perspective Compression

Explanation

We recreated this same effect by taking two pictures of a long street, one zoomed in from a distance and one close up without zoom. This time, zoomed-in picture appears flattened because the longer focal length compresses the perspective, making objects appear closer together than they actually are. On the other hand, the uncompressed image, taken with a shorter focal length, preserves the real spatial relationship between objects.

Part 3: The Dolly Zoom

In this part, I duplicate a classic film effect called the dolly zoom. It is a camera movement also known as the "Vertigo shot," after a famous scene in Hitchcock's Vertigo.
